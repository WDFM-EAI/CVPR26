1st Workshop on Deployment of Foundation Models for Embodied AI

June 2026

WDFM-EAI @ CVPR 2026

Denver, June 2026

The 1st Workshop on Deployment of Foundation Models for Embodied AI (WDFM-EAI) aims to advance the efficient, scalable, and safe deployment of large foundation models in embodied intelligence systems — spanning autonomous driving, robotics, and general-purpose embodied agents.

Building on the success of our previous Workshops on Distillation of Foundation Models for Autonomous Driving at CVPR & ICCV 2025, which focused on deploying foundation models for self-driving systems, this new edition broadens the scope to holistically address embodied AI from both wheeled (autonomous driving) and legged (robotic) perspectives. The workshop explores synergies between these domains, emphasizing how shared challenges in perception, reasoning, and latency can be jointly addressed through the deployment of multimodal foundation models such as Vision-Language-Action Models and World Models.

The workshop will cover a wide range of topics, including but not limited to:

  • Multimodal Perception for Embodied AI
  • Real-Time Decision Making with Foundation Models
  • Vision-Language-Action Models in Embodied AI Systems
  • World Models for Planning and Control
  • Model Compression and Distillation for Autonomous Systems
  • Robustness and Safety in Multimodal Autonomous Systems
  • Generative Models for Simulation and Training
  • Cross-Domain Applications of Foundation Models in Embodied Platforms

Keynote Speakers

Building an Autonomous Future

Ashok Elluswamy / Tesla
Autonomous driving represents one of the most challenging applications of embodied AI, requiring real-time perception, prediction, and planning at scale. This talk will explore how foundation models and large-scale neural networks are being deployed in production autonomous systems. We will discuss the engineering challenges of deploying vision-language-action models in safety-critical applications, including model compression, latency optimization, and robustness guarantees. The talk will cover Tesla's approach to building end-to-end neural networks for autonomous driving, from multi-camera vision to trajectory planning, and the lessons learned from deploying these systems to millions of vehicles worldwide.

End-to-End Motion Forecasting and Distillation

Dragomir Anguelov / Waymo
Motion forecasting is a critical component of autonomous driving systems, requiring accurate prediction of multi-agent behaviors in complex urban environments. This talk will present advances in end-to-end learning approaches for motion forecasting, where large foundation models learn to predict future trajectories directly from sensor inputs. We will discuss how knowledge distillation techniques enable deploying these powerful models efficiently on edge devices while maintaining prediction accuracy. The presentation will cover Waymo's research on transformer-based architectures for multi-agent prediction, the challenges of training and deploying these models at scale, and recent advances in distilling complex forecasting models into production-ready systems.

Frontiers in Embodied AI for Autonomous Driving

Alex Kendall / Wayve
Embodied AI represents a paradigm shift in autonomous driving, where agents learn to drive through end-to-end learning from sensor data to control signals. This talk will explore the frontiers of embodied AI for autonomous driving, focusing on how foundation models can be adapted and deployed for real-world driving tasks. We will discuss the challenges of learning robust driving policies from diverse data, the role of world models in planning and simulation, and how vision-language-action models can enable more generalizable autonomous systems. The presentation will cover Wayve's approach to end-to-end learning for autonomous driving, including recent advances in foundation model architectures, data efficiency, and deployment strategies that enable safe and scalable autonomous systems.
